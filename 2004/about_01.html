What is Designing Matter?

Designing Matter is a major new collaborative learning experiment at the University of Virginia involving students, faculty, and administrators from numerous schools, departments, disciplines, programs and initiatives. Together we are building bridges between academic cultures and developing a common language for discussing issues of importance to us all. For undergraduates from the College, and the Architecture and Engineering Schools who are enrolled in session and discussion components of the project, Designing Matter is CCSC 200, a course for credit in the university. As students plan individualized projects and engage in experiential and service learning, connections with the local community will increase as well. We also hope to involve alumni and the broader community, real and virtual, through our website and other networks and materials that emerge from our project over time. From the very start, we begin with the assumption that our findings and educational model are to be shared.
