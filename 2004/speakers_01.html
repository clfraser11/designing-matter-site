Speakers    >     Brooks Pate

Prof. Brooks Pate currently acts as a Professor of Chemistry at the University of Virginia, where he also received his B.S. in 1987. After earning his Ph.D., at Princeton University, Prof. Pate went on to become the recipient of numerous awards and fellowships, such as the Camille and Henry Dreyfus New Faculty Award, 1993; the NSF Career Award, 1996; the Coblentz Award, 1999; and the MacArthur Fellowship, 2001, among others.

The Pate Research Group studies the dynamics of molecules with significant amounts of vibrational energy and the kinetics of energy flow in molecules. A major emphasis in his laboratory is the spectroscopy of molecules as the intramolecular vibrational energy redistribution (IVR) process, and possibly reaction, occurs. In particular, Pate is interested in how coherent excitation of highly excited molecules can be used to influence reaction products. The Pate Research Group has developed a new type of molecular spectroscopy called dynamic rotational spectroscopy to study isomerization reactions of isolated molecules. They also investigate the vibrational dynamics of molecules in dilute solution.
