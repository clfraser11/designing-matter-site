Undergraduate Project Consultants    >     Brenda Goguen

Brenda Goguen is a third year undergraduate double majoring in chemistry with a biochemistry specialization and biology. She is currently conducting research in Professor Fraser's lab, where she is involved in collaboration with Prof. Michael Lawrence in the Biomedical Engineering Department to synthesize peptide-tagged polymers for use as a treatment for chronic inflammation. She was awarded a GlaxoSmithKline Undergraduate Summer Research Fellowship and a UVa Summer Research Grant (2003) for this project, which integrates organic chemistry with cell biology. When Brenda attended Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology in Alexandria, VA and for two summers thereafter, she worked in a genomic laboratory in the Department of Environmental Science and Policy at George Mason University in Manassas, VA. Brenda used DNA fingerprinting and sequencing to examine microbial communities in water and soil. Her project won the 2001 Stockholm Junior Water Prize and allowed her to travel to Stockholm, Sweden to compete in an international competition. Through this opportunity, Brenda has been able to promote water awareness and conservation. With her research at UVa and at George Mason, she has experience writing competitive proposals and formal scientific papers. Also she is currently the treasurer of the UVa Biology Society and is involved in Madison House volunteering. Her other interests are in art and in how the sciences and humanities can be integrated.
