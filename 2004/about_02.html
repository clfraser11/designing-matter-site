Course Description

Designing Matter, CCSC 200, is a Common Course centered in the sciences that explores our relationship to matter from subatomic to cosmic scales. Fields such as laser interactions with matter, molecular design and synthesis, nanotechnology, human genetics, cell biology and regenerative medicine, perception and cognition, computers and graphic design, art, architecture, ecology, environmental policy, and astronomy will be represented.

Both the material life cycle and design process will be considered in weekly sessions featuring distinguished speakers from across the disciplines. Those concerned with seeing, shaping, representing, and understanding matter—scientists, engineers, MDs, artists and architects—will be joined by scholars from the humanities and social sciences in a comparative study of scientific exploration and the implications of matter distribution, manipulation and utilization for human experience and society.

Weekly readings will accompany presentations on Tuesdays from 5:30-7:30 pm. Students will also consider course themes in one-hour discussion sections in Pavilion VIII on the Lawn and in 2-3 short writing assignments. Sections will also function as workshops to assist students in developing independent projects, documenting their experience in "lab notebooks", and communicating them persuasively in research proposals that serve as the major assignment for the course.

Grants will be awarded to individuals or collaborative teams submitting the most creative proposals so that they might implement their research plans. This three-credit course contributes to the fulfillment of the science requirements in the College.
