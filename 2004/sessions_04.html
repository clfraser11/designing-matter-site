February 10, 2004:   Nanoscience, Technology and Ethics

Readings

Ratner and Ratner, Nanotechnology: A Gentle Introduction to the Next Big Idea, Chapters 1-3 required. Rest of book recommended.

Ratner and Ratner, Nanotechnology and Homeland Security: New Weapons for New Wars. Last chapter (protected: part 1, part 2).

Lab Book

Consult four websites with the word "nano" in their title. See Nanosys and Nanosphere and then pick two others. Write a very short critique of each of these Websites based on four criteria:

  • Is the project on which the company is based scientifically realistic?
  • Do you think that the products that the company will produce will have an appropriate market?
  • Are there ethical concerns involved with what the companies are proposing?
  • Would you invest your own money in this company?

Questions

The gray goo question - Bill Joy, retired from the computer industry, has written concerning a possible scenario in which self replicating nanobots could invade our bloodstreams and make us their captives - Michael Crichton's new book entitled Prey is built around this same theme, and there was a very interesting recent exchange between Richard Smalley and Eric Drexler on this subject.

Judged on the basis of what you have learned about nanoscience, is the gray goo scenario (self replicating nanoscale robots that would enter the human blood stream, colonize it and make us captives) realistic? Could it happen within a reasonable time scale? Should research into all nanoscience and nanotechnology stop because of concerns about this?

Is nanotechnology more like biotechnology, information technology or the industrial revolution? Describe why.

Do chemistry and/or physics limit the sorts of molecular assembly that can in fact be produced?  For example, is it possible to place twenty carbon atoms in a small sphere around one carbon atom, with a distance between each pair of carbons equal to twice the radius of a carbon atom?

Describe, and give a citation of some sort to, one nanoscience or nanotechnology project being carried out in the state of Virginia.
