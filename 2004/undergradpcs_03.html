Undergraduate Project Consultants    >     Sachin Gupta

Sachin Gupta is a fourth year undergraduate student pursuing a B.A. in ethnomusicology. He enthusiastically serves as an Undergraduate Project Consultant for the Designing Matter project. His interdisciplinary major in ethnomusicology stems from research interests in intercultural musics, international development, international public health, and the socio-cultural issues surrounding the use of music in non-Western healing traditions.

Supported by a Center for Global Health University Scholar Award, he spent the summer of 2002 conducting an ethnomusicological study of music's role in healing at three research sites in Ecuador. In the northwest Andes of Ecuador, he examined how shaman Don Segundo Maigua used ayahuasca, a psychotropic mixture, along with chanting and the harmonica to spiritually enact healing. In addition, he studied how shaman Don José Quimbo of Peguche formed his own, unique method of healing based on specifically applying various flute-like instruments to heal different parts of the body. Lastly, he briefly spent time with Fundación S.I.N.A.M.U.N.E., a music school for handicapped, poor children (ages 6-12) in the capital city of Quito run by the famous Ecuadorian trumpeter, Edgar Palácios. He presented his findings at the Fall 2002 Undergraduate Research Symposium and wrote a 65-page ethnography under the guidance of Professor Edie Turner in the Department of Anthropology.

In the summer of 2003, he returned to Ecuador with the assistance of a Harrison Undergraduate Research Award to study the structure and methodologies of Health Promotion Using Music (PROSAMUSA), a program founded by Dr. Benjamín Puertas at the Universidad San Francisco de Quito. He spent the majority of his time in Carpuela, a little-studied highland Afro-Ecuadorian community, studying the community's distinctive musical practices and health issues. Based on this research, he worked (and is still working) to build support for bringing PROSAMUSA to Carpuela and surrounding, underserved Afro-Ecuadorian communities in the Chota Valley. The project is supported by Fundación Coangue, an incipient non-profit organization that undertakes projects primarily to aid Chota Valley communities.

Currently, Sachin is focusing his senior thesis on the question of if and how health promotion can be enacted in a "culturally-sensitive" manner in communities that have non-Western beliefs regarding health and illness. He also interns 10-15 hours per week at the International Rescue Committee's Charlottesville chapter, where he helps refugees from various countries (e.g. Liberia, Afghanistan, Bosnia, Colombia) restart their lives in Charlottesville. After graduation, he hopes to spend a year in the Chota Valley of Ecuador continuing his work with PROSAMUSA and Fundación Coangue. He then looks forward to attending medical school and later obtaining either a Master of Public Health degree or an M.A. in Anthropology.
