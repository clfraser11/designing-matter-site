The Teaching Team

Cassandra Fraser (Chemistry) serves as project coordinator, along with four graduate teaching assistants (GTAs) who have been recruited from across the disciplines—Ray Malewitz from the English Department (with background in chemistry and environmental engineering), Jennifer Aultman, (Anthropology, with research in archeology, and background in physics and environmental sciences), Rima Franklin (Environmental Sciences, with research on microbial ecology with a molecular biological approach), and Michael Dunn, from the Urban Planning Department in the Architecture School (with background in Mechanical Engineering, and and co-inventor of an all-wheel drive bicycle, the product now sold by a company he helped launch).

Five undergraduate project consultants (UPCs) will also provide support: Scott Geyer, Brenda Goguen, Sachin Gupta, Laura Juliano, and Aaron Miracle. These students too, have stellar records in the sciences and strong track records in project design and execution. They have traveled abroad, speak many languages, and have won prestigious national and even international awards.

The teaching team will engage in discussion with students about course themes, but also will help them to identify interests, devise plans for exploring them, and communicate them persuasively in proposals. Additionally, the teaching team will serve as the jury or review panel to select five students' proposals for funding. Students will be encouraged to dig deeply into a topic, to think big, to collaborate if they wish, to pursue experiential and service learning projects, and to think about how they might share their findings and excitement with others in the university and community.

Together, the students, teaching team and presenters will see what we can accomplish, in terms of thinking in new ways, broadening our perspective, and generating new ideas and project plans. Additional support is also provided by Dean Ayers and Lori Schuyler, his Assistant, the Teaching Resource Center, Victor Luftig, TNE Coordinator, Dean of Students Penny Rue with expertise on service learning, and Randy Bell, Science Education Professor at the Curry School of Education. Liz Pisciotta serves as Project Assistant and Steve Johnson is the Web Developer.
