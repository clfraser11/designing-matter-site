Undergraduate Project Consultants    >     Scott Geyer

Scott Geyer is a third year chemistry and physics major, with an emphasis on biological applications of these fields. He also brings interest and expertise in math and computer science to problems that are of benefit to society. Much of Scott's work has centered around the use of computers to simulate or solve physical systems or equations, for example modeling DNA structure and using quantum chemistry to look at the dynamics of a novel drug compound. For example, he conducted research on the effects of metal ion binding on DNA structure under Dr. Sabat of the Chemistry Department at UVA. His computational skills and knowledge of quantum mechanics have been applied to the development of new course materials, namely movies for Dr. Sundberg's textbook website. And last summer he conducted research in computational biology at the NSF-NIH Bioinformatics Bioengineering Summer Institute in Pittsburg. There, he analyzed sequence specific DNA bending agents for use in a variety of disease treatments. Scott is a member of the Undergraduate Research Network's technology committee and he has served as a speaker and presented his research in the program's Brain Food series. Along with interdisciplinary experience across the sciences, from biology to chemistry, physics, math and computers which he is eager to share, Scott also brings to the Undergraduate Project Consultant role a strong interest and curiosity about the humanities as well as extensive peer leadership experience gained through participation in clubs and scouts.
