Undergraduate Project Consultants    >     Aaron Miracle

Aaron Miracle is a third-year biochemistry major, primarily interested in science: biology, chemistry, and virology. He has maintained a fascination with HIV since high school and currently is involved in virology/microbiology research at the Myles H. Thaler Center for AIDS and Human Retrovirus Research in the Microbiology Department in the medical school at UVA. He has earlier experience in molecular biology gained during an internship in the Molecular Signaling Transduction Section at National Institute of Allergy and Infectious Disease (NIAID) at the NIH. More recently, Aaron has become involved with "Promoting-HIV-Negativity", addressing the health education aspects of HIV and taking a particular interest in the epidemic in China. As a member of the LGBT Speaker's Bureau Aaron concentrates on public education, and his interest in China is derived from experiences living in Taiwan for 10 years. Additionally, Aaron has held offices in club badminton and played club volleyball. He plans to apply to Medical Science Training programs (MD/PhD) in the fall, and hopes to attend residency in internal medicine and ultimately specialize in infectious disease.
